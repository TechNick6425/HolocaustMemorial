"Thou shalt not be a victim, thou shalt not be a perpetrator, but, above all, thou shalt not be a bystander."

- Yehuda Bauer

This quote means that no one should stand and watch something like the Holocaust happen, and that you shoul do something about it.
"Do you know why most survivors of the Holocaust are vegan? It's because they know what it's like to be treated like an animal."

- Chuck Palahniuk, Lullaby

This quote means that the camp inmates were treated very poorly, and that they don't want to eat meat, because they know how badly animals are treated.

- Gene Simmons

This person is trying to tell us that we don't really understand the Holocaust, and that we only know it as a word.
"As the Nobel laureate Elie Wiesel warned years ago, to forget a holocaust is to kill twice."

Iris Chang, The Rape of Nanking

This means that if you forget a holocaust, it will happen again.
"Escape was not our goal since it was so unrealistic. What we wanted was to survive, to live long enough to tell the world what had happened in Buchenwald."

- Jack Werber, Saving Children: Diary of a Buchenwald Survivor and Rescuer

This person is saying that they weren't trying to escape because it was impossible, instead trying to stay alive until they got liberated.
"The duty of the survivor is to bear testimony to what happened...You have to warn people that these things can happen, that evil can be unleashed. Race hatred, violence, idolatries - they still flourish."

- Elie Wiesel

This quotes is saying that if you survived something horrible, that you should tell the story of it, so that it doesn't happen again, and yyou can prevent another person from experiencing it.
"The Holocaust illustrates the consequences of prejudice, racism and stereotyping on a society. It forces us to examine the responsibilities of citizenship and confront the powerful ramifications of indifference and inaction."

- Tim Holden

This quote is telling us that the Holocaust is a result of personal hatreds against a group of people, and that we need to solve these.
"Fiction cannot recite the numbing numbers, but it can be that witness, that memory. A storyteller can attempt to tell the human tale, can make a galaxy out of the chaos, can point to the fact that some people survived even as most people died. And can remind us that the swallows still sing around the smokestacks."

- Jane Yolen

This quote tells us that art can't provide a way for us to understand the Holocaust, but a survivor can.
"The past is a presence between us. In all my mother does and says, the past continually discloses itself in the smallest ways. She sees it directly; I see its shadow. Still, it pulses in my fingertips, feeds on my consciousness. It is a backdrop for each act, each drama of our lives. I have absorbed a sense of what she has suffered, what she has lost, even what her mother endured and handed down. It is my emotional gene map."

- Fern Schumer Chapmen, Motherland: Beyond the Holocaust: A Mother-Daughter Journey to Reclaim the Past

This person's mother had a difficult time, because she experienced the Holocaust. It was always weighing her down she was trying to do something.
"Between two throw-ins in a soccer game, right behind my back, three thousand people had been put to death."

- Tadeusz Borowski, This Way for the Gas, Ladies and Gentlemen

This person is saying that the people of Germany had no idea the Holocaust was happening. It was just business as usual.
